Rainbow weather in Chicago

Chicago sees its rainbows with changeable, showery weather: spring and autumn cold fronts, summer thunderstorms that clear near evening. Bows here reward attention: they follow the app's classic pattern of rain ending while low sun breaks through.

At 41.9° north, Chicago's useful viewing window shifts with the seasons. Local winter brings longer periods of low sun, while summer usually concentrates the geometry near sunrise and sunset.

Wherever you are, the geometry is the same: a primary rainbow forms opposite the sun while the sun is less than about 42 degrees above the horizon. Rainbow questions, answered

When do rainbows appear in Chicago?
Rainbows need sunlit rain with the sun lower than about 42 degrees, so in Chicago useful windows often occur after sunrise or before sunset on showery days. A study in ZhaoSu, China found that about nine in ten observed rainbows occurred during the hour after rainfall. Bowcast uses that regional finding as research guidance, not a universal rate.
Where should I look for a rainbow in Chicago?
Always directly opposite the sun: put the sun at your back and look toward the retreating rain. In the evening that means looking east; in the morning, west.
