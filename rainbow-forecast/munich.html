Rainbow weather in Munich

Munich sees its rainbows with changeable, showery weather: spring and autumn cold fronts, summer thunderstorms that clear near evening. Bows here reward attention: they follow the app's classic pattern of rain ending while low sun breaks through.

Wherever you are, the rules are the same: a rainbow forms opposite the sun, only while the sun is less than 42 degrees above the horizon, and most often in the minutes after a shower moves through. The gauge above runs those rules against live ensemble forecasts for the 12 km around Munich.

Rainbow questions, answered

When do rainbows appear in Munich?
Rainbows need sunlit rain with the sun lower than 42 degrees, so in Munich the best windows are the hours after sunrise and before sunset on showery days. Around nine in ten rainbows appear in the hour right after rain stops.
Where should I look for a rainbow in Munich?
Always directly opposite the sun: put the sun at your back and look toward the retreating rain. In the evening that means looking east; in the morning, west.
