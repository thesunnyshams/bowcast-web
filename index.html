A rainbow is geometry, physics, and meteorology.

Geometry fixes the rainbow at 42 degrees from the point opposite the sun. Weather then decides if sunlit rain is sitting on that patch of sky. Bowcast pulls this data through live ensemble forecasts from Open-Meteo and turns them into a probability through a Monte Carlo over forty members. Each threshold is drawn from published atmospheric-optics research: Carlson et al. (2022), Liu et al. (2023), and Businger (2021).
